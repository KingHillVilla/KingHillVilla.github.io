Automated owner's report

The same scheduled "founder's report" we run on our iOS app, pointed at the villa. Claude runs on a schedule (say every Monday) and emails a digest, with zero manual work:

  • Arrivals & departures this week
  • New inquiries, with draft replies ready to send
  • New Airbnb / Google reviews + sentiment
  • Open dates worth promoting
  • Occupancy & revenue vs. last month
  • Maintenance and turnover reminders

The pattern: connect the accounts once, then Claude handles the repetitive work on a schedule, the inbox triage, the guest emails, the weekly report, so the owner spends time on guests instead of admin. It is the difference between running the rental and the rental running you.

Ed's drone footage: getting it on the site

Ed's top ask is an opening drone video like his friend's site. Two easy paths, and the best result uses both together:

Option A: YouTube or Vimeo (easiest)
  • What Ed does. Upload the full drone tour to YouTube or Vimeo (both free) and send the link.
  • We embed it in the Film section. No file hosting or bandwidth limits, works on every device, and they handle the compression.
  • Best for the longer 1 to 3 minute property tour. Vimeo looks cleaner with no ads; YouTube is free and familiar.
Option B: Hero video loop (the "wow" opening)
  • The effect Ed loves: a short, silent, looping aerial that plays the instant the page opens, behind the title.
  • What Ed picks. One 10 to 20 second clip, a slow push over the cliffs or a rise revealing the villa and pool.
  • We compress it to roughly 5 to 8 MB and set it to autoplay, muted, and looping. Fast to load, no controls, pure atmosphere.

Recommended combo: the short looping clip as the hero (the instant cinematic open, like his friend's site) plus the full tour embedded just below in the Film section. All Ed has to do is send the raw clips or a YouTube link. Claude trims, compresses, and wires them in. It is a same-day change once the footage arrives.
